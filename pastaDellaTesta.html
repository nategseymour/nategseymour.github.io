Pasta della Testa

Ingredients
24oz (or more) fresh tomatoes
6 (or more) cloves garlic
1 box high quality pasta
1 block tasty cheese (or cheese equivalent)
1 handful fresh basil, chopped


Get a bunch of good tomatoes--the multicolor cherry ones work great, but fresh off the vine is good too--or a mix! Cut them all into 1 or ½-inch pieces and toss in a bowl with a splash of olive oil and a little bit of salt.

Pop open a handful of garlic cloves and peel. Cut the buttholes off but leave whole otherwise.

Bring a large pot of well-salted water to boil (taste the water to be sure it’s salted enough)

Heat a deep, tall-sided pan or crock pot. Add 1/4 inch olive oil and wait a moment until oil is shimmering hot but not quite smoking. Toss garlic in and fry until the garlic is fragrant and golden brown. Quickly remove and reserve garlic using a fork or tongs, leaving the oil in the pot.

Add the tomatoes to the garlic-infused oil and stir gently. Cook tomatoes down (stirring occasionally) until a sauce begins to form. Crush any larger chunks with a wooden spoon if needed. This will take at least 30 minutes.

Meanwhile, once cooled, mince the fried garlic and save for later.

Also meanwhile, cook pasta in the large pot until nearly al dente (it’ll cook a little bit more in the sauce). Reserve 1 cup of pasta water. Drain pasta, do not rinse.

Once the tomato mixture is nice and thicc, stir the minced garlic back into the pot. Add the cooked pasta to the sauce pot and gently toss with tongs. Add a splash of reserved pasta water if everything is getting too dense.

BOOM. Now you have a delicious pasta dish. Add cheese (or equivalent) and basil and serve.

This is a flexible one and is easy to iterate upon depending on what you have on hand. I like to use a separate pan to cook up some sliced mushrooms and onions and add them to the sauce once it’s in the thickening stage. You can also brown some impossible/beyond beef on the side and add to the sauce. Diced black olives and capers will give it a puttanesca vibe. Etc etc have fun.
